**Card structure (top to bottom, all center-aligned):**
1. Heart icon — `<Icon name="decorative" size={24} />` in coral-400 or coral-300, placed just above or beside the name for warmth
2. Name — text-heading-xl or text-display size, text-text-primary, the person's name rendered large ("Dear [Name]" energy)
3. Thin horizontal divider — 1px line, use cream-400 or blue-200 color, roughly 60% card width, centered, with vertical margin for breathing room
4. Compliment body — text-body-lg, text-text-secondary, the compliment text with comfortable line-height
5. Footer — "EgoBoost 3000" in text-label size, text-text-muted, low opacity (~60%), at the bottom with generous top margin

**Card container styling (all locked decisions):**
- Background: bg-cream-50 (warmest cream, almost white — gives paper/stationery feel)
- Shadow: Use a generous soft shadow for the "floating card" effect. CSS-only approach. Example: `shadow-[0_8px_30px_rgba(90,74,111,0.10)]` (uses text-primary hue for shadow color to stay on-palette). Tune as needed.
- Border radius: rounded-card (20px token)
- No visible border (per user decision)
- Padding: Use DLS card padding tokens via inline style `style={{ padding: 'var(--spacing-card-pad-y) var(--spacing-card-pad-x)' }}` (Tailwind v4 does not generate padding utilities for custom spacing tokens)
- Portrait/vertical proportions: Set a max-width of ~360-400px and let height be auto (content-driven). The vertical feel comes from the narrow width + padded content.
- Center on page: The card itself is a block — centering is handled by the parent container (mx-auto or flex justify-center)

**Paper/stationery texture (Claude's discretion):**
Use a subtle CSS-only technique to give the card a slight paper feel. Options: a very faint background-image noise pattern using CSS gradients, or a subtle inner shadow that creates depth. Keep it minimal — the cream color + shadow already do most of the work. If the technique adds complexity for minimal visual gain, skip it and rely on the cream + shadow alone.

**Text sizing for different compliment lengths (Claude's discretion):**
Use a single fixed text size (text-body-lg) for the compliment. Do NOT implement shrink-to-fit — it adds complexity and the Caveat font handles wrapping naturally. Long compliments will simply wrap to more lines.

**Integration with kitchen sink:**
Add a new "Compliment Card" section to KitchenSinkScreen.tsx AFTER the existing Icons section. Follow the established section pattern:
- `<section className="mb-12">`
- `<h2 className="text-heading-md text-text-secondary mb-6">Compliment Card</h2>`
- Render the card centered within the section, on a small blue-200 or blue-100 background pad to simulate the real page context (cream card on blue surround)
- Use placeholder props: name="Mx. Spectacular" compliment="You are a mass of radiant sunshine wrapped in human form. Scientists are baffled. Poets weep. Even your houseplants are thriving."

**What NOT to do:**
- Do NOT add any border to the card (user locked: soft shadow only, no visible border)
- Do NOT add corner decorations, patterns, or flourishes beyond the heart icon and divider (user locked: minimal decoration)
- Do NOT use any hardcoded color values — everything from DLS tokens
- Do NOT import from Google Fonts or any CDN (self-hosted Caveat only)
- Do NOT implement any animation or transition on the card itself
